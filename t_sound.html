techniques of cinema

sound

Everybody knows that first there was silent film and then there was sound. Without sound a movie would have very little meaning. It would not grab the audience’s attention or evoke their emotion, it would just be a dragging motion picture of people talking. Therefore, sound is used extensively in filmmaking to enhance presentation, and is distinguished into two broad type, namely diegetic and nondiegetic sound:

Diegetic sound

It is sound that the characters can hear as well as the audience, and usually implies a reaction from the character. Diegetic sound is also called "literal sound" or "actual sound". These include voices of characters, sounds made by objects in the diegesis (like the heartbeat of a person), basic sound effects (like a dog barking, a car passing, the radio playing, or a character coughing), and source music i.e. sounds coming from instruments (record players, radios, tape players etc.) in the diegetic space.

The following clip from The Dark Knight (2008) has instances of diegetic sound, which include the voices of the Joker, Batman, and other characters.

Non-diegetic sound

It is sound which is represented as coming from a source outside the story space, i.e. its source is neither visible on the screen, nor has been implied to be present in the action. Non-diegetic sound is also called "non-literal sound" or "commentary sound". These include a narrator's commentary, Voice of God, sound effect added for dramatic effect, mood music, and film score. Non-diegetic sound plays a significant role in creating the atmosphere and mood within a film.

College & Electric Youth's song A Real Hero is the background score, and a non-diegetic sound, in this scene from Drive (2011).

Voice Over

When a voice, often that of a character in the film, is heard while we see an image of a space and time in which that character is not actually speaking. The voice over is often used to give a sense of a character’s subjectivity or to narrate an event told in flashback. It is overwhelmingly associated with genres such as film noir, and its obsessive characters with a dark past. It is usually pre-recorded in a studio and then played over in the film.

An example of a voice-over can be found in the following clip from Sunset Boulevard (1950), introducing us to the eponymous boulevard and providing an insight into what we are seeing.

Theme Music

A recognisable piece of music written specifically for a film and is usually played during the intro, opening credits and/or ending credits. The purpose of a theme song is often similar to that of a leitmotif.The theme music for the franchise of Ghostbusters (1984) and Jurassic Park (1993) are among the most recognisable and popular ones.

The theme music from Ghostbusters (1984) is arguably one of the most famous ones that is instantly recognisable.

Exaggerated/Pleonastic sound

Directors often use exaggerated sound to heighten emotion or meaning in a scene. Using sound in this way can suggest an incoming threat before the audience sees it. It can be used to reinforce a character’s threatening nature when they are present on screen. It can also help to emphasise the emotional impact of a specific action taking place on screen.

The sound of Chris's (Daniel Kaluuya) heartbeat is exaggerated in this clip from Get Out (2017) to underscore his fear and trepidation.

Sound effects

Sound effects and background noises (like the sound of footsteps and rain falling) are integral in giving a sense of reality to the world depicted in a film. Sound effects can equally be used to increase mystery, suspense and disorientation for the viewer, like in the science fiction and horror genres.

The sound of rainfall is a sound effect that adds to the sombre feel in the following clip from John Wick (2014) that takes place on the day of a funeral.

Foley Sound

Foley sound takes its name from Jack Foley, a sound editor at Universal Studios. Foley sound is live action sound recreated in a studio, using a variety of different everyday objects and surfaces, like the sound of drawing on paper with pencil.
