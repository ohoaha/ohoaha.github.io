main genres of cinema

crime

An extremely wide-ranging group of fiction films that have crime as a central element of their plots. The fictionalized criminal act, however, is only a point of departure in defining this group of films.The criminals or gangsters are often counteracted by a detective-protagonist with a whodunit plot. Hard-boiled detective films reached their peak during the 40s and 50s (classic film noir), although have continued to the present day. Therefore, crime and gangster films are often categorized as film noir or detective-mystery films, and sometimes as courtroom/crime legal thrillers - because of underlying similarities between these cinematic forms. This category also includes various 'serial killer' films. The old media adage, "If it bleeds, it leads," describes a common narrative approach in cinema.

1. Crime action

A hybrid of action and crime films and a subgenre of action films as well. Most films of this kind fall in the category of heist films, prison films and sometimes cop and gangster films. Car chases and shootouts are commonly featured.

Notable examples of this subgenre are The Dark Knight (2008), Baby Driver (2017), Master (2016), Collateral (2004), and Heat (1995).

2. Crime comedy

A hybrid of crime and comedy films. Mafia comedy looks at organized crime from a comical standpoint. Humor comes from the incompetence of the criminals and/or black comedy.

Some crime comedy movies are Analyze This (1999), The Pope of Greenwich Village (1984), Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels (1998), Snatch (2000), In Bruges (2008), Tower Heist (2011), and Pain & Gain (2013).

3. Crime drama

Crime dramas are, as the name suggests, a combination of crime and drama.

Straight Time (1978) and Badlands (1973) are two crime drama films.

4. Crime thriller

A thriller in which the central characters are involved in crime, either in its investigation, as the perpetrator or, less commonly, a victim (generally, this is just a thriller). While some action films could be labelled as such for merely having criminality and thrills, the emphasis in this genre is the drama and the investigative/criminal methods.

Examples of crime thrillers include Silence of the Lambs (1991), Heat (1995), Seven (1995), Witness (1985), Memories of Murder (2003), and The Call (2013).
